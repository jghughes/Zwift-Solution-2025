1 zFTP: Zwift Functional Threshold Power (W/kg). zFTP metrics are displayed, but play no role in computations.
2 Pull: Duration, power, and ratio to zFTP for each rider's main pull. Pull abilities are calibrated from 90-day best power graphs on ZwiftPower. Riders with superior pull power are prioritised for longer pulls. Stronger riders are at the front and rear of the paceline, protecting weaker riders in the middle.
3 NP: Normalized Power.
4 IF: Intensity Factor (NP as % of one-hour power).
5 Limit: Pull plan logic is such that all riders get to pull, and paceline speed is constant. In a no-drop plan, speed is governed by the pulling power of the weaker rider/s and the IF % they or other rider/s suffer. Sprinters are relatively stronger for short pulls, but weaker for longer pulls - the opposite is true for endurance riders. Pulling power is unrelated to zFTP.
